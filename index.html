Process

HOW IT
WORKS

01
Staff Spots an Issue

A bartender notices a patron is over-intoxicated, a floor host spots an altercation, or a guest reports harassment. They open Flagged.

02
One Tap to Flag

Select an alert type, add optional location detail, and hit send. The whole process takes under 3 seconds — no typing required.

03
Security Gets Notified

Every on-duty bouncer receives an instant push notification. They see the alert type, location, and who flagged it. They acknowledge with one tap.

04
Incident Is Logged

Once resolved, staff close the incident. The full chain — who flagged it, who responded, time to resolution — is permanently stored and exportable.

Liability Protection

YOUR LEGAL
SHIELD

"Thorough documentation practices can make or break your defense against over-service claims."

— Hospitality Liability Law, 2024

When a dram shop lawsuit hits, the question is always the same: what did your staff know, and what did they do? Without Flagged, your answer is "we think someone said something around midnight." With Flagged, you have a timestamped, named, legally exportable record of every decision made that night.

  • TX Texas TABC allows a safe harbor defense — but only if you can prove your staff followed responsible service protocols. Flagged is your proof.
  • CA California holds venues liable for serving obviously intoxicated persons. Courts look for documented evidence of staff awareness and action.
  • NY New York dram shop law allows third-party injury claims. Contemporaneous incident records are the gold standard for defense.
  • FL Florida's strict liability standards make documented responsible service essential — especially for late-night venues and nightclubs.
